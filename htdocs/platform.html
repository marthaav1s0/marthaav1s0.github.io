Platform




Kalayaan sa COVID Plan

  • To push for sufficient wages and benefits for health workers immediately, along with sufficient financial support for hospitals.
  • Healthcare will be free and accessible. Leni Robredo said that within three months, Filipinos should be enrolled in the newly-created National Health Insurance Program as envisioned in the Universal Health Care Act. This includes fixing the Philippine Health Insurance Corporation by creating an audit team for the agency.
  • She will also push for the following:
    1. Free COVID-19 testing.
    2. Sufficient vaccines for Filipinos by making sure there are funds for it.
    3. Increasing the capacity of hospitals.
    4. A nurse of each barangay.
    5. Expanding telemedicine consultations nationwide for safety of doctors and lessen COVID-19 exposure. This plan is similar to the Office of the Vice President’s free teleconsultation service E-Konsulta.
    6. Free and safe mobile testing system for communities with high COVID-19 cases.
    7. Filipinos should receive aid and lockdowns should only be implemented in targeted areas.
  • To set aside P216 billion for cash aid and to make use of the National ID system in the immediate distribution of aid.

Recovery Plans

  1. Business
    • Promised support for any type of enterprises, allocating a budget of P100 billion to keep workers employed. She said she will also push for a National Unemployment Insurance Program with laid-off workers receiving 80% of their salary but will have a fixed budget of P192 Billion which will not allocate new funds, but we will identify how we will spend our money to programs that will give greater convenience to our citizens.
    • Strengthening agriculture and fisheries by doubling the Department of Agriculture’s budget, so that every sector in the industry will not be left behind.
  2. Education
    • Aims to push better access to education and opening up schools in low-risk areas, while providing gadgets and free internet access in high-risk areas. And pushing for additional community learning hubs nationwide.
    • Also part of her plan is allocating a P68-billion education aid where each student will be provided P300 per month or P3,000 per year that will be used for purchasing load or printing of modules. She promised that the government has the resources to implement her ambitious plan.

Hanapbuhay para sa Lahat (5 point recovery plan)

  1. Regaining trust in government
    • Eradicating corruption and red tape so that the playing field for businesses should be leveled.
    • Reviving the National Competitiveness Council that would ensure the Philippines remains globally competitive
    • Strengthening Philippine Competition Commission and the Ease of Doing Business Act to end unfair business practices.
  2. Reviving the industries
    • Prioritizing the 4 industries: maritime, climate, technology, and manufacturing.
  3. Ending discrimination at work
    • Implement the Anti-Age Discrimination Act, but would still push for a comprehensive anti-discrimination bill.
  4. Supporting small businesses
    • Passing the National Food Security bill that will allow the government to buy from small farmers and fisherfolk for feeding programs. She promised to link businesses to wider markets, especially in agriculture and tourism.
  5. Saving jobs
    • To create a Public Employment Program which would guarantee jobs to around 2 million Filipinos.
